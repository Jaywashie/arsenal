Est. 1886 · North London

140 Years of Gunners Glory

Arsenal Football Club was founded in 1886 as Dial Square by workers at the Royal Arsenal in Woolwich, south-east London. They turned professional in 1891 and relocated to Highbury in 1913, establishing themselves as one of English football's most storied institutions. Under legendary manager Herbert Chapman in the 1930s, the Gunners became the dominant force in the country, winning five First Division titles and setting standards that still define the club.

The modern era brought Arsène Wenger, whose 22-year reign transformed Arsenal into a global brand. His 1997–98 and 2001–02 Double-winning sides were breathtaking, but it was the 2003–04 "Invincibles" — an entire league season unbeaten — that etched Arsenal into football folklore forever. Mikel Arteta arrived in 2019 and, after a patient rebuild, guided a fearless young squad back to the summit of English football.

2025–26 Premier League Champions

After three agonising runner-up finishes, Mikel Arteta's Arsenal ended a 22-year wait for the Premier League title in the 2025–26 season — confirmed with a game to spare when Manchester City could only draw at Bournemouth. The Gunners finished with 82 points, winning 25 of 37 games and keeping 19 clean sheets, with goalkeeper David Raya claiming the Golden Glove for a third straight season. It is Arsenal's 14th English top-flight title and their fourth in the Premier League era.
